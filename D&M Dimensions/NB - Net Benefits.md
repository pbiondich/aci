# NB — Net Benefits

Part of the [[DeLone and McLean IS Success Model]]

## Definition
Net Benefits captures the **downstream, tangible outcomes** generated by using the ambient AI system. It asks: what concrete value did this produce — for clinicians, patients, and the organization?

## What Belongs Here
- Documentation time (objective, clock-measured)
- After-hours documentation (work outside work)
- Chart closure timeliness
- Total EHR time
- Patient experience and relationship quality
- Physician-patient interaction quality (face time, attention)
- Clinical patient safety outcomes
- Financial productivity (RVU / revenue impact)
- Coding accuracy and completeness (ICD-10, HCC, E/M)
- Patient volume and throughput

## What Does NOT Belong Here
- Provider *feelings* about time savings (→ [[US - User Satisfaction]])
- Note quality (→ [[IQ - Information Quality]])

## ⚠️ Key Cross-Cutting Findings

**Documentation Time (CM-04):** 30 papers measure this. Reductions range −12% (EHR telemetry) to −72% (self-report). Self-report consistently overestimates. EHR audit-log is the most reliable method.

**After-Hours (CM-05):** Consistent null pattern in objective data (Pearlman 2025, Stults 2025). Self-report shows improvement. Exceptions: Duggan 2025 (−16.9%), Boyter/KLAS 2025 (−39%), Afshar 2025b (fragile, sensitive to outliers).

**Patient Experience (CM-17):** Almost entirely unmeasured with validated instruments. Only Owens 2024 uses PDRQ-9 — null result in masked phase.

**Patient Safety (CM-19):** Castro 2025 found more psychiatric documentation but less psychiatric intervention (aOR 0.83) — the only real-world safety signal in the corpus. Preprint only.

**Financial Productivity (CM-20):** Three independent papers show RVU/revenue improvement. PHTI 2025 warns that even accurate higher coding increases aggregate healthcare spending at scale.
