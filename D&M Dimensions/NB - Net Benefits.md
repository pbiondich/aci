# NB — Net Benefits

Part of the [[DeLone and McLean IS Success Model]]

> **Note:** In this review, Net Benefits has been split into two separate dimensions following the original DeLone & McLean (1992) model: [[II - Individual Impact]] (clinician-level outcomes) and [[OI - Organizational Impact]] (system- and patient-level outcomes). This split better reflects the distinct stakeholder levels relevant to clinical AI evaluation. This file is retained for historical reference and to document the rationale for the change.

## Original Definition
Net Benefits captured the downstream, tangible outcomes generated by using the ambient AI system, for clinicians, patients, and the organization. In the 2003 D&M update, Individual Impact and Organizational Impact were collapsed into this single dimension.

## Why We Split It
The 2003 consolidation was motivated by e-commerce contexts where individual and organizational impacts were difficult to disentangle. In healthcare, they are distinct and should not be collapsed:
- Clinician outcomes (time savings, burnout, fulfillment) accrue to the individual provider
- Patient outcomes and organizational outcomes (financial, safety, throughput) accrue at the system level
- Conflating them obscures the finding that patient outcomes are almost entirely unmeasured in this corpus

See [[II - Individual Impact]] and [[OI - Organizational Impact]] for the current active dimensions.
