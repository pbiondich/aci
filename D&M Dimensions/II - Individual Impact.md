# II — Individual Impact

Part of the [[DeLone and McLean IS Success Model]]

> This dimension restores the original DeLone & McLean (1992) Individual Impact construct. It was collapsed into [[NB - Net Benefits]] in the 2003 update but is reinstated here because clinician-level and organizational-level outcomes are meaningfully distinct in healthcare AI evaluation.

## Definition
Individual Impact captures the effect of the ACI system on the individual clinician's work experience, time allocation, and cognitive state. It asks: what concrete value did this produce for the clinician using the system?

## What Belongs Here
- Documentation time (objective, clock-measured)
- After-hours documentation (work outside work)
- Chart closure timeliness
- Total EHR time
- Clinician burnout and exhaustion
- Cognitive and task load
- Professional fulfillment and meaningfulness

## What Does NOT Belong Here
- Provider *feelings* about time savings (→ [[US - User Satisfaction]])
- Note quality (→ [[IQ - Information Quality]])
- Patient outcomes (→ [[OI - Organizational Impact]])
- Financial and system-level outcomes (→ [[OI - Organizational Impact]])

## ⚠️ Key Cross-Cutting Findings

**Documentation Time (CM-04):** 30 papers measure this. Reductions range −12% (EHR telemetry) to −72% (self-report). Self-report consistently overestimates. EHR audit-log is the most reliable method.

**After-Hours (CM-05):** Consistent null pattern in objective data (Pearlman 2025, Stults 2025). Self-report shows improvement. Exceptions: Duggan 2025 (−16.9%), Boyter/KLAS 2025 (−39%), Afshar 2025b (fragile, sensitive to outliers).

**Burnout (CM-01):** Only one RCT (Afshar 2025b) confirms reduction (−0.44 PFI, p<.001). QI studies show larger effects driven by selection bias. Burnout and professional fulfillment are dissociable: burnout reduces without a corresponding increase in fulfillment.
