# DM-07 Equity of Impact Across Roles

**Status:** Draft — pending key informant validation
**D&M Dimension:** [[NB - Net Benefits]]

## Definition
Whether ACI adoption benefits different clinical roles equitably — including attending physicians, residents/trainees, advanced practice providers (NPs, PAs), nurses, and medical assistants — or whether it primarily benefits one group while adding burden, displacement, or workflow disruption to others.

## Hypothesized Stakeholder(s)
- **CPO** — responsible for the wellbeing of the entire clinical workforce, not just physicians; inequitable impact creates organizational tension
- **COO** — workflow changes that shift burden to other roles (e.g., nurses doing more note cleanup, scribes being displaced) have operational implications

## Rationale
The corpus is heavily physician-centric. Most satisfaction, burnout, and efficiency measures are collected from physicians or physicians plus APPs. The impact on nurses, medical assistants, and existing human scribes is almost entirely unmeasured. If ACI reduces physician documentation burden but increases MA intake documentation requirements, or displaces human scribes without transition support, the net organizational impact may be different from what physician-only data suggests.

## Relationship to Existing Canonical Measures
- [[CM-01 Clinician Burnout and Exhaustion]] — measured almost exclusively in physicians; unclear whether other roles experience burnout changes
- [[CM-15 User Satisfaction]] — satisfaction data rarely includes non-physician roles
- [[CM-13 Adoption Behavior and Utilization Rate]] — adoption patterns may differ by role

## Suggested Measurement Methods
| Method | Description | Data Source |
|---|---|---|
| Role-stratified satisfaction survey | Satisfaction and workflow impact measured separately for physicians, APPs, nurses, MAs | Survey |
| Workflow redistribution analysis | Pre/post mapping of documentation-related tasks across all roles in the care team | Time-motion study |
| Scribe displacement tracking | Outcomes for human scribes after ACI adoption (redeployment, termination, role change) | HR records |
| Role-specific burden assessment | Task burden changes measured by role, not just by documentation time | Survey + observation |

## Key Questions for Validation
- Has ACI adoption affected non-physician staff workflows in your organization?
- Were human scribes displaced, and if so, how was that transition managed?
- Do you measure ACI impact on nursing or MA workflows?
- Would evidence of inequitable impact across roles change your deployment strategy?
