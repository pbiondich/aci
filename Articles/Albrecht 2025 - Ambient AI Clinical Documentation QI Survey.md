# Article Review

---

## Article Title
Enhancing clinical documentation with ambient artificial intelligence: a quality improvement survey assessing clinician perspectives on work burden, burnout, and job satisfaction

## Author(s)
Albrecht, Michael; Shanks, Denton; Shah, Tina; Hudson, Taina; Thompson, Jeffrey; Filardi, Tanya; Wright, Kelli; Ator, Gregory A.; Smith, Timothy Ryan

## DOI
[10.1093/jamiaopen/ooaf013](https://doi.org/10.1093/jamiaopen/ooaf013)

## Journal / Source
JAMIA Open | Vol. 8, No. 1 | 2025. Open Access. University of Kansas Medical Center (KUMC). Abridge-affiliated co-author (Tina Shah). Received July 11, 2024; Accepted February 7, 2025.

## Ambient Product(s) Evaluated
**Abridge** (explicitly named). Ambient AI documentation platform. iPhone/Android app records conversations; generates structured notes that can be edited and inserted into Epic via dot phrases. Partial integration during study (not full Epic mobile app to note automation). 30 specialties represented; grouped into primary care, medical subspecialty, surgical subspecialty for analysis.

## Clinical Setting
University of Kansas Medical Center (KUMC). 181 clinicians initially offered access; 133 "adopters" (completed ≥5 encounters) offered post-survey. **Pre-survey:** 93/181 (51.9% response). **Post-survey:** 99/133 (74.4% response). Pre-survey anonymous; post-survey not. Median days using Abridge before post-survey: 92 days (IQR 66-172). 87% physicians, 13% APPs. Demographics of post-survey respondents: median age 41, median years in practice 12, median years at institution 4.

## Evidence Level
Quality Improvement (pre/post survey study)

## Data Source(s)
Pre/post surveys (14 items total across both surveys). Pre: 8 items on baseline documentation experience. Post: 11 items on perceived impact. Several concordant questions analyzed together using proportional odds logistic regression (POLR). Covariate analysis for specialty type and days of use.

## Article Summary
This QI study at KUMC assessed clinician perceptions before and after implementing Abridge ambient AI documentation across 30 specialties. Key quantitative findings: clinicians had **6.91 times higher odds** of reporting easier documentation workflow post-implementation (95% CI 3.90-12.56, p<.001) and **4.95 times higher odds** of agreeing they could complete notes before the next patient (95% CI 2.87-8.69, p<.001). In the post-survey, 81% agreed the tool made workflow easy, 77% reported decreased documentation burden, 73% reported decreased after-hours documentation, 67% reported reduced burnout risk, and 64% reported increased work satisfaction. 47% agreed they could add at least one more patient if needed. Specialty type and days of Abridge use did not significantly affect responses. Pre-survey showed high baseline documentation burden (83% spent time outside clinical hours, 75% at risk for burnout). The study is notable for including medical and surgical subspecialists (not just primary care) and showing consistent benefit across specialties.

## Hypothesis
To evaluate the impact of Abridge ambient AI documentation on clinicians’ perceptions of documentation burden, after-hours work, burnout risk, and job satisfaction.

*📍 Notable: University of Kansas Medical Center study with Abridge-affiliated co-author (Tina Shah). Multi-specialty (30 specialties grouped into primary care/medical/surgical). Consistent benefit across specialties — contrasts with Prasad 2025 where primary care benefited less.*
*📍 Strong self-reported improvements: 81% found workflow easier, 77% reported decreased burden, 73% less after-hours work, 67% reduced burnout risk. 47% felt they could add a patient if needed.*
*📍 Methodological note: Pre-survey anonymous, post-survey not. Pre-survey response rate 51.9%, post 74.4%. POLR analysis used for concordant questions. No validated burnout instrument — self-report only.*
*📍 Implementation context: 73.5% adoption rate among those offered access (133/181 completed ≥5 encounters). Median 92 days of use before post-survey. Benefit seen relatively quickly.*
*📍 COI: Tina Shah (Abridge) is a co-author. Disclosed in article. Similar pattern to Olson 2025 (Abridge co-author) and Palm 2025 (Suki authors).*
*📍 Practical impact: Study supports ambient AI as a solution for documentation burden across specialties, though authors call for future studies using validated instruments and comparing platforms.*
