## Article Title
The impact of nuance DAX ambient listening AI documentation: a cohort study

## Author(s)
Haberle, Tyler; Cleveland, Courtney; Snow, Greg L.; Barber, Chris; Stookey, Nikki; Thornock, Cari; Younger, Laurie; Mullahkhel, Buzzy; Ize-Ludlow, Diego

## DOI
[10.1093/jamia/ocae022](https://doi.org/10.1093/jamia/ocae022)

## Journal / Source
Journal of the American Medical Informatics Association (JAMIA) | Vol. 31, No. 4 | 2024 | pp. 975–979. Intermountain Health. Cohort study with peer-matched controls.

## Ambient Product(s) Evaluated
**Nuance Dragon Ambient eXperience (DAX)**. Ambient listening/digital scribing solution. iOS app captures ambient natural conversation; sent to Microsoft/Nuance cloud for conversational AI parsing; generative AI-derived draft note reviewed by human quality reviewers before being pasted into provider's EHR note for final edit and signature.

## Clinical Setting
Intermountain Health (33-hospital integrated health system across 7 states). Study completed in subset of Intermountain Medical Group clinics in Utah using Oracle Cerner EHR. 99 providers across 12 specialties enrolled; 76 matched control group providers included for analysis. March–September 2022.

## Evidence Level
Observational (peer-matched controlled cohort study)

## Data Source(s)
Press Ganey Workforce Engagement surveys | Patient LTR scores | EHR metrics (after-hours EHR time, documentation time per patient) | Documentation and CPT deficiency rates | wRVU productivity | Attributed VBC panel sizes | Patient opt-out counts | Nuance daily turnaround time reports

## Article Summary
This cohort study from Intermountain Health evaluated Nuance DAX ambient listening AI documentation across 99 providers representing 12 specialties with 76 matched controls. Median DAX utilization was 47% among active participants. Key findings: positive trends in provider engagement (higher scores in engagement, safety, resilience, and work-life balance compared to all employed adult primary care providers); no quantifiable effect on patient safety or LTR scores; small but statistically significant increase in productivity (wRVU); no meaningful change in VBC-attributed panel sizes; statistically significant worsening of after-hours EHR time; no significant impact on 24-hour documentation or CPT submission deficiency rates. Expert internal audit found no meaningful evidence of over-coding, missed risk-adjustment, or insufficient supporting documentation. Authors note this is the first published study describing the use and practical outcomes of ambient listening AI documentation in real-world outpatient clinical care at modest scale.

## Hypothesis
To evaluate the impact of implementation of Nuance DAX on provider engagement, productivity, panel size, documentation (including time spent in the EHR after hours), and coding timeliness. Also measure impact to patient safety and LTR by patients.

*📍 First published study describing use and practical outcomes of ambient listening AI documentation in real-world outpatient clinical care at modest scale (99 providers, 12 specialties).*
*📍 Peer-matched controlled cohort study design with 76 matched controls. Strong methodology for observational study.*
*📍 Positive trends in provider engagement; no quantifiable effect on patient safety or LTR scores; small productivity increase of no practical significance; statistically significant worsening of after-hours EHR time (contrary to other studies).*
*📍 No meaningful change in documentation timeliness or CPT submission deficiency rates. Expert audit found no over-coding or insufficient documentation.*
*📍 Utilization: Median 47% among active participants. 9 providers (9.1%) stopped using DAX completely; 32 (32.3%) had license removed due to low utilization. "Intention to treat" approach may dampen positive effects.*
*📍 Authors note this may be first published study of ambient listening AI in real-world outpatient care. Calls for head-to-head comparisons of EHR documentation efficiency training.*
*📍 Intermountain Health context — 33-hospital integrated system across 7 states. Study completed in subset of Utah clinics using Oracle Cerner EHR.*
