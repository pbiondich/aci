# Article Review

---

## Article Title
Ambience Healthcare ROI Validations 2025: How the Pursuit of Clinician Well-Being Led to Demonstrable ROI for St. Luke's Health System

## Author(s)
Boyter, Mac; Blauer, Tyson (KLAS Research authors)

## DOI
None — grey literature (KLAS Research ROI Validations Series report)

## Journal / Source
KLAS Research | ROI Validations Series | June 2025

## Ambient Product(s) Evaluated
**Ambience Healthcare** — ambient AI documentation platform deployed at St. Luke's Health System. Generates clinical documentation and associated diagnostic and billing codes (including HCC capture and E/M coding) in providers' natural Epic workflow.

## Clinical Setting
Ambulatory — St. Luke's Health System. 1,005 beds, $3.2B annual patient revenue, Epic EHR. 600 total Ambience users (600 active). 41 specialties. 5-week implementation timeline, on budget. April 2023–June 2024 measurement period (E/M analysis).

## Evidence Level
Grey Literature (KLAS Research single-site case study; not peer-reviewed)

## Data Source(s)
- Epic User Action Log (UAL) — objective documentation time telemetry (n=371 providers)
- Structured pilot surveys: pre-onboarding, 60-day, 180-day post-implementation (n=47 from 100-provider pilot group)
- Claims/billing: E/M coding CPT data + CMS reimbursement rates; HCC v28 mapping (ICD-10)
- Financial ROI: revenue cycle and finance team data

## Article Summary
KLAS Research ROI Validations Series case study of Ambience Healthcare at St. Luke's Health System. Uniquely rigorous for a grey literature study: uses Epic UAL telemetry data (n=371), structured longitudinal surveys (n=47), and claims/billing analysis. Key findings: 36-percentage-point decrease in near-daily burnout; 41% reduction in active documentation time; 39% decrease in after-hours documentation time; increased HCC capture worth $9,685/clinician/year; E/M complexity improvement worth $1,907/clinician/year; E/M volume increase worth $1,456/clinician/year; **total financial benefit: $13,049/clinician annually**; 41% reduction in time to chart closure.

## Hypothesis
Not explicitly stated. Goal: document quantitative and qualitative ROI achieved by St. Luke's Health System following Ambience Healthcare implementation.

## Key Quantitative Findings

### Clinician Benefits
- **Burnout:** −36 percentage points in near-daily burnout (n=47 pilot group)
- **Documentation time:** −41% in active documentation time (UAL, n=371)
- **After-hours:** −39% in after-hours documentation time; −17% documenting after appointment date

### Financial Benefits
- **HCC capture:** Increased capture translating to **$9,685/clinician/year** in additional revenue
- **E/M complexity:** Increased E/M level complexity → **$1,907/clinician/year**
- **E/M volume:** Increased daily E/M visit volume → **$1,456/clinician/year**
- **Combined financial benefit: $13,049/clinician/year**

### Employee Satisfaction (Qualitative)
- 81% agree/strongly agree: Ambience improves job satisfaction (n=47)
- 75% agree/strongly agree: more energy at end of workday due to decreased cognitive load
- Qualitative themes: improved work-life balance, renewed connection to patients, reduced burnout

### Operational
- **Chart closure:** −41% reduction in time to chart closure

## Methodology Notes

- **Epic UAL data** is objective telemetry — stronger than self-report time estimates. This is one of the more rigorous data sources in the grey literature tier.
- **n=371 for UAL/financial analyses** — substantial sample for a single-site study
- **n=47 for burnout/satisfaction surveys** — from the 100-provider pilot group only
- E/M analysis methodology was rigorous: CPT → E/M level mapping, case mix normalization, baseline/expansion comparison
- HCC methodology: ICD-10 → HCC v28, collapsed duplicates, excluded hierarchical replacement, normalized for visit volume — conservative approach
- **KLAS caveat explicitly stated:** "This report reflects the experience of one customer site and is not a comprehensive view of the vendor's broader customer base."

## COI Assessment

- **KLAS ROI Validations Series:** KLAS is an independent healthcare IT research firm, but vendors must already be KLAS-rated and must be in "high-energy markets." Ambience was selected as subject — vendor engagement in KLAS data programs exists.
- St. Luke's chose Ambience — no randomization; self-selected adopters. Selection bias likely.
- No explicit funding conflict disclosed beyond standard KLAS methodology.
- This is meaningfully more rigorous than vendor-produced case studies — KLAS maintains methodological standards.

## D&M Analysis

- **NB (Net Benefits):** Most financially detailed paper in corpus. $13,049/clinician/year = strongest ROI claim with most rigorous methodology (UAL + claims data). Compare to Holmgren 2026 ($3,044/year via RVU analysis). The difference may reflect HCC capture being Ambience-specific vs. RVU analysis being product-agnostic.
- **IQ (Information Quality):** HCC capture and E/M coding improvement are IQ-adjacent findings — the tool is capturing more clinical information leading to better coding. This indirectly validates note completeness.
- **US (User Satisfaction):** 81% job satisfaction improvement; 75% more energy at end of day. Strong.
- **U (Use/Adoption):** 600 users, 41 specialties — large enterprise deployment. 5-week implementation.

## Corpus Context

- **Financial productivity comparison:** Holmgren 2026 (UCSF, DAX) = +$3,044/physician/year. Boyter/KLAS (St. Luke's, Ambience) = +$13,049/clinician/year. Large difference — likely attributable to HCC capture coding improvements being a major driver at St. Luke's (behavioral health + complex patients may have more HCC opportunity).
- **Documentation time:** −41% in active documentation time (UAL) vs. Ma 2025 (−12% per note, DAX, Stanford). Different products, methodologies, and definitions. Ambience's number is higher but uses UAL, not per-note telemetry.
- **After-hours null reversal:** This paper shows −39% after-hours time (UAL data), contrasting with the "after-hours null" pattern in Pearlman 2025, Stults 2025 (self-report vs. objective divergence). UAL methodology here may be more sensitive to real changes.
- **Ambience-specific:** This is the only paper in corpus specifically evaluating **Ambience Healthcare** (as opposed to DAX, Abridge, Suki, Nabla).
