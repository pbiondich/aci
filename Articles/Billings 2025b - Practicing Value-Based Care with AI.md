## Ambient Product(s) Evaluated
**Navina AI Copilot** — AI assistant focused on value-based care workflows: synthesizes fragmented patient data into problem-oriented summaries, surfaces HCC coding opportunities, care gaps, and risk adjustment support at the point of care. **This is NOT primarily an ambient documentation/dictation scribe** — it is a pre-visit/during-visit AI assistant for VBC complexity management. Navina does include ambient documentation as a roadmap item but was evaluated here primarily as a VBC copilot.

## Clinical Setting
Primary care — value-based care settings. 120 physicians across 19 practices, 13 US states. Mix of small independent practices, multi-site organizations, and large medical groups with varying VBC experience. VBC performance metrics drawn from 3,100+ physicians across the 19 organizations.

## Article Summary
Phyx Primary Care (nonprofit innovation lab) evaluated Navina's AI copilot in VBC-focused primary care settings. 120 physicians surveyed after ≥30 days of use; VBC performance metrics from 3,100+ physicians across 19 organizations. Key findings: 40% reduction in clinical review time for complex visits, 32% burnout reduction (validated against MBI), significant improvements in RAF scores (+0.153) and STAR quality ratings (+1.9 points), 91% HCC recommendation address rate, 92% physician trust in HCC suggestions. Study limitations: no control group, retrospective design, reliance on self-report, selection bias (willing adopters). COI concern: Navina provided the KPM data; study partially co-designed with Navina as "Innovation Lab Partner."

## Key Findings (Full Text)

### Burden Reduction
- **40% decrease** in average clinical review time for complex visits (10.0 → 6.0 min) — p=0.869, **NOT statistically significant**
- **24% drop** in self-reported chart review burden (p=0.000, significant)
- **12% increase** in visits where physicians felt fully prepared (p=0.002, significant)

### Physician Well-Being
- **32% reduction in burnout** (47% → 32%, p=0.023) — measured using Non-Proprietary Single Measure of Burnout, validated against Maslach Burnout Inventory
- **23% decline in dissatisfaction** (22% → 17%, p=0.005)
- After-hours workload: −6% (2.2 → 2.1, p=0.204) — **NOT statistically significant**
- Care Time Rating: +5% (2.7 → 2.8, p=0.174) — **NOT statistically significant**

### Adoption & Trust
- 90% used the Assistant weekly
- 94% found it easy to access and use
- 92% said it helped capture more diagnoses
- 90% said it supported HCC coding
- 92% trusted HCC suggestions; 85% trusted diagnosis prompts
- 64% said it gave them hope for their practice and primary care overall

### VBC Performance (vendor-provided KPMs, 3,100+ physicians, 19 organizations)
- **2.8 average HCC codes captured per patient**
- **91% of HCC recommendations addressed; 73% accepted**
- **+0.153 increase in RAF score**
- **+1.9 point gain in STAR quality scores** across 32 domains (23 HEDIS + 9 MSSP/AWV)

### Non-Recommenders (8%)
- Acknowledged benefits but noted increased documentation steps under VBC thoroughness expectations
- "The workload hasn't gone down — it's just shifted."
- Reflects the tool's effectiveness at surfacing diagnoses while VBC itself remains demanding

### Limitations (explicitly acknowledged by authors)
- No control group — cannot attribute changes solely to AI
- Retrospective design — susceptible to bias
- Reliance on physician surveys — recall bias and social desirability bias
- Selection bias — willing adopters may be more tech-open or efficiency-motivated
- KPM data provided by Navina (vendor) — not independently audited

## D&M Analysis

- **U (Use):** 90% weekly usage, high adoption rate among those who committed ≥30 days
- **US (User Satisfaction):** 32% burnout reduction (p=0.023) ✅; dissatisfaction −23% (p=0.005) ✅; care time and after-hours NOT significant. High trust scores. 84% recommend.
- **NB (Net Benefits):** Review time reduction NOT significant (p=0.869). Fully-prepared visits improvement significant (p=0.002). RAF +0.153 and STAR +1.9 are the most compelling outcomes — but based on vendor-provided data without independent audit.
- **SerQ (Service Quality):** VBC workflow integration as the central implementation design. MEAT-compliant documentation. Multi-source data synthesis.
- **IQ (Information Quality):** HCC coding accuracy supported — 91% addressed, 73% accepted. Not a documentation note quality study.
- **SQ (System Quality):** Not assessed — no transcription accuracy or system reliability measures.

## Corpus Context

- **Same evaluator as Billings 2025a** (Phyx Primary Care, Edmund Billings MD). Consistent methodology, consistent COI concerns. Both funded by the "AAFP Innovation Lab" with vendor as "Lab Partner."
- **Navina ≠ ambient documentation scribe.** Unlike Suki, DAX, Abridge — Navina is a pre/during-visit VBC AI copilot. If this is included in the corpus, it represents a category expansion beyond ambient documentation to AI-assisted VBC workflow broadly.
- **The 40% clinical review time reduction (p=0.869) is the most cited headline finding — and is NOT statistically significant.** This is a methodological red flag that should be flagged in any synthesis citing this paper.
- **Burnout finding (−32%, p=0.023) is the strongest result** and aligns with Billings 2025a's similar finding, but both share the same evaluator and similar methodology — they are not independent replications.
- **After-hours reduction null (p=0.204)** — consistent with the corpus-wide pattern of after-hours being null on objective measures.
- **COI:** Navina provided the KPM data and is named as "Innovation Lab Partner." The authors acknowledge use of ChatGPT and Gemini in drafting the report. Not peer-reviewed.
