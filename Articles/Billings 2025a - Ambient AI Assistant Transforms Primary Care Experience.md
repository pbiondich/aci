# Article Review

---

## Article Title
Ambient AI Assistant Transforms the Primary Care Experience

## Author(s)
Billings, Edmund MD; Waldren, Steven E. MD MS (Phyx Primary Care)

## DOI
None — grey literature (Phyx Primary Care Innovation Lab report; not peer-reviewed)

## Journal / Source
Phyx Primary Care Report | February 2025
Published: phyxprimarycare.org | © Phyx Primary Care, Inc. 2025

## Ambient Product(s) Evaluated
**Suki AI** — described by Phyx as an "AI Clinical Assistant" (explicitly distinguished from "Ambient AI Scribes" which Phyx frames as simpler/less integrated products). Phyx selected Suki after assessing companies in the category; chosen for voice/AI technology, mission alignment, primary care focus, scalability, safety compliance, and EHR integration capability.

## Clinical Setting
Primary care — 116 primary care physicians surveyed across 37 practice locations nationwide. Mix of small independent practices, primary care organizations, and large health systems. ~80% small/independent. All respondents had used Suki AI Assistant for **more than 30 days** prior to survey.

## Evidence Level
Grey Literature (non-profit innovation lab report; survey-based; not peer-reviewed)

## Data Source(s)
Survey/self-report — Phyx Primary Care Vital Signs® (proprietary validated instrument); online survey; Net Promoter Score; 3 open-ended qualitative questions

## Article Summary
Phyx Primary Care (nonprofit innovation lab) evaluated Suki AI's impact on 116 primary care physicians across 37 locations using their proprietary Primary Care Vital Signs® survey instrument. All respondents had used Suki for >30 days. The survey assessed burnout, practice satisfaction, scheduled visit time, adequacy of patient care time, and after-hours EHR time. Key findings: 60% fewer physicians reporting burnout; 81% more reported practice satisfaction; 41% reduction in notetaking time; 27% reduction in documentation burden; 44% reported improved patient interactions "to a great extent"; 20% could increase patient care time during visit; 33% spent less time after-hours. Report frames Suki as an "AI Clinical Assistant" vs. simpler "Ambient AI Scribes."

## Hypothesis
Not explicitly stated. Lab aim: evaluate whether primary care physicians' use of an AI Assistant can reduce burnout by improving Primary Care Vital Signs®, and inform adoption decisions.

## Key Findings

- **60% fewer physicians reported burnout** after using Suki AI (Primary Care Vital Signs® single-item burnout, validated against MBI)
- **81% more physicians felt satisfied** with their practice
- **41% reduction in notetaking time** (self-report)
- **27% reduction in documentation burden** (self-report)
- **>50% said Suki improved note quality "to a great extent"**
- **44% said Suki improved patient interactions "to a great extent"**
- **33% spent less time working after hours**
- **20% were able to increase time spent on patient care during visits**

Context from report: Phyx references the 2024 Annals of Family Medicine finding ("More Tethered to the EHR") that for every 8 hours of patient visits, primary care physicians spend 5.3 hours in the EHR, with 2.1 hours on clinical documentation. This frames the problem Suki is addressing.

## Methodology Details (from full text)

- **Primary Care Vital Signs®** is Phyx's proprietary instrument. It includes:
  - **Non-Proprietary Single Measure of Burnout** — psychometrically tested against the Maslach Burnout Inventory (MBI). This is a meaningful methodological claim: it's not just a custom Likert, it's validated against the gold-standard burnout instrument.
  - Physician satisfaction
  - Scheduled visit time
  - Adequacy of time for patient care
  - EHR time spent after hours
- **>30 days of Suki use** required before survey — not a fresh-adoption measurement
- **Online survey** with quantitative + 3 open-ended qualitative questions
- **Net Promoter Score** included
- **37 practice locations** nationwide; mix of independent, primary care organizations, health systems

## Phyx's Conceptual Framework

Phyx explicitly distinguishes two product categories:
1. **AI Clinical Assistants** (e.g., Suki): Full AI platform with EHR integration, interoperability, roadmap to broader assistance (inbox, ordering, medication reconciliation, coding). More expensive but designed for robust, long-term assistance.
2. **Ambient AI Scribes**: Simpler products designed to replace in-person/virtual scribes, minimal integration, no broader assistance roadmap. Less expensive.

Phyx frames Suki in Category 1. This distinction is vendor-adjacent and may reflect Phyx's relationship with Suki.

## COI and Limitations

- **Phyx-Suki relationship:** Phyx explicitly states they "assessed companies in this category to find a partner for the lab" and chose Suki. They also note they have conducted prior labs with Suki. While Phyx states they "do not receive any funding or support from lab partners," the prior relationship and Suki-favorable framing should be noted.
- **All self-report:** No objective EHR telemetry data. All outcome measures are self-reported.
- **>30 days of use** means this is a population that has already adopted and continued — survivor bias; those who found it unhelpful likely stopped before 30 days.
- **Proprietary instrument:** Primary Care Vital Signs® is Phyx's own tool. Even though the burnout item is validated against MBI, the overall instrument is not independently validated in peer-reviewed literature.
- **No control group; no baseline pre-post design.** Survey asks retrospectively about change, not pre/post measured at two timepoints.

## D&M Analysis

- **US (CM-01 Burnout):** 60% reduction in burnout reports. The single-item burnout measure is validated against MBI — stronger than many corpus papers that use fully custom Likert items. But survivor bias and no control group are significant limitations.
- **US (CM-15 Satisfaction):** 81% increase in practice satisfaction; NPS captured.
- **NB (CM-04 Documentation Time):** 41% notetaking reduction — self-report. Compare to objective telemetry: Ma 2025 found −12%, Boyter/KLAS −41% (UAL). The convergence with Boyter/KLAS is notable, but both are non-RCT.
- **NB (CM-05 After-Hours):** 33% reported less after-hours time — self-report only.
- **NB (CM-18 Interaction Quality):** 44% improved patient interactions "to a great extent"; 20% increased face-to-face patient care time.

## Corpus Context

- **Only one of two Suki-specific papers** in corpus (along with AAFP 2021). Both are grey literature with Phyx-Suki relationship. Neither provides objective telemetry data.
- **Small/independent practice focus:** 37 locations, ~80% small/independent — the most underrepresented setting in the corpus. This is unique and valuable for understanding ambient AI outside academic health systems.
- **Product framing distinction:** Phyx's "AI Clinical Assistant" vs. "Ambient AI Scribe" taxonomy is not used elsewhere in the corpus. Suki frames itself differently from DAX/Abridge/Ambience.
- **Burnout instrument strength:** MBI-validated single item is stronger methodology than many QI studies in the corpus. But no objective data, no control group.
