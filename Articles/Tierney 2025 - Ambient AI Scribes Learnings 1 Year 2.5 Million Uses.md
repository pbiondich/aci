## Article Title
Ambient Artificial Intelligence Scribes: Learnings after 1 Year and over 2.5 Million Uses

## Author(s)

Two unnamed ambient AI scribe vendors — The Permanente Medical Group (Kaiser Permanente) transitioned between vendors during the study period. Both vendors unnamed throughout. This is the 1-year follow-up to Tierney 2024 (initial pilot, also in corpus).

## Article Summary
One-year operational follow-up of ambient AI scribe deployment at TPMG/Kaiser Permanente, building on Tierney 2024 (10-week pilot). Covers Oct 16, 2023 – Dec 28, 2024 (63 weeks). 7,260 physicians used the tool across 2,576,627 patient encounters. Usage grew linearly; top-third high users drove 89% of total activations. Dose-response confirmed: high users saved 0.7 min/note vs. 0.15 min/note for low users. Aggregate savings: 15,791 hours (~1,794 8-hour working days) vs. nonusers. DiD analysis shows significant reductions in pajama time, after-hours work, notes time, and orders time; small increase in in-basket time. Physician survey: 84% positive impact on visit interactions, 82% positive work satisfaction. Patient survey: 56% positive impact on visit quality, 0% negative, 92% comfortable or neutral with AI. Organization switched vendors mid-period; adoption remained stable, with prior usage patterns predicting subsequent usage. No COI — entirely industry-independent TPMG report.

## Hypothesis
Not formally stated (commentary format). Key questions investigated: (1) Is AI scribe use growing/sustainable? (2) Does dose-response exist? (3) What physician characteristics predict adoption? (4) What is the physician and patient experience? (5) What happens when you switch vendors?

## Key Findings (Full Text)

### Adoption & Growth
- **63 weeks** (Oct 16, 2023 – Dec 28, 2024): 7,260 TPMG physicians used AI scribe across **2,576,627 patient encounters**
- **3,447 physicians** used it in ≥100 encounters; top single user: ~7,000 encounters
- Usage grew **linearly** throughout the period
- Average user: **47 years old**, **19 years** post-training — neither age nor years since graduation associated with higher usage (**challenges generational adoption assumptions**)
- Users tended to be a **higher proportion of women**, but this was confounded by high-adoption specialties (e.g., primary care) having more women physicians
- Highest adoption specialties (% of visits): **mental health 50%, EM 36%, primary care 34%**
- Highest adoption (% of clinicians): **mental health 42%, primary care 32%, EM 32%**; followed by allergy 21%, cardiology 20%
- *"The highest adoption rates occurred in departments that typically suffer from the highest levels of documentation burden and burnout."*

### Dose–Response
- **High users** = top third by volume; accounted for **89% of all activations**
- High users had MORE time in notes/orders/clinical review per appointment **before** deployment (selection effect: heaviest-burdened physicians adopted most)
- Dose-response for time in notes: **high users −0.7 min/note; low users −0.15 min/note** vs. nonusers (Fig. 3)
- After Vendor 2, high users were spending **significantly less time in notes than all other physicians** — a reversal from baseline
- Specialty high-user rates: mental health 55%, primary care 47%, EM 37%; lowest: infectious disease 0%, OB/GYN 6%, urology 9%

### Documentation Workload (DiD Analysis, Oct 2023 – Dec 2024, users vs. nonusers)
| Metric | DiD (min saved) | P-value |
|---|---|---|
| Pajama time | −1.03 | 0.02 |
| Time outside 7am–7pm | −1.83 | <0.001 |
| Time in notes/appointment | −0.40 | <0.001 |
| Time in orders/appointment | −0.07 | 0.01 |
| Time in in-basket/appointment | **+0.10 (increase)** | 0.008 |

- **Aggregate time savings in notes: 15,791 hours = ~1,794 8-hour working days** vs. nonusers over 1 year

### Physician Survey (n=102 Adult & Family Medicine physicians)
- **66%** used AI scribe ≥5 days/week; 12% → 4 days; 5% each → 3, 2, 1 day; **8% never used**
- **63%** used it in every in-person visit; 29% in most visits
- Lower usage in telemedicine: 44% every phone visit, 41% every video visit
- **84% felt AI scribes had a positive impact on visit interactions**
- **82% reported positive effect on work satisfaction**; 2 physicians reported "somewhat worse" effect
- Barriers cited: no template integration (n=3), typing faster than editing (n=2)

### Patient Survey (n=118 patients from adult/family medicine panels; 116 recalled AI scribe used)
- **39%** reported doctor spent more time speaking with them; 56% no difference; **5% less time**
- **47%** reported doctor spent less time at computer; 51% no difference; 2% more time
- **56% said AI scribe had a positive impact on visit quality; 44% no impact; 0% negative**
- **92% comfortable or neutral** with AI in their appointment; **8% reported some discomfort**
- Some patients with "no change" still endorsed continued use if it helped their doctor

### Vendor Transition
- TPMG switched vendors in 2024 for two reasons: (1) enterprise-wide KP rollout (all 8 regions) necessitated vendor reevaluation; (2) new vendor offered **direct EHR integration**
- **3,182 new users** adopted only with Vendor 2 (i.e., never used Vendor 1)
- **2,686 physicians** used both vendors
- Vendor 2 initial adoption slope was **steeper** than Vendor 1 in first 23 weeks (Fig. 8)
- Prior usage predicted future usage: **78% of high users stayed high; 50% of low users stayed low; 17% of low users became high** with new vendor
- Behavior is **largely vendor-agnostic** — habits transfer across platforms

### Limitations
- Single large integrated health system (results may differ elsewhere)
- Vendor transition prevented direct matching of usage data across full period
- Could not evaluate note editing proportion over full period (only available early)
- Physician and patient surveys not linked to demographic data — cannot analyze differential experience by sociodemographic group
- Future work needed: qualitative analyses, health equity dimensions, patients with disabilities (speech impairment), trust

## D&M Analysis

- **U (Use/Adoption):** Linear growth to 2.5M uses in 63 weeks. Sustained and growing. High users drive 89% of volume (Matthew Effect). Adoption is specialty-driven, not age-driven. Vendor-agnostic habits.
- **US (User Satisfaction):** 84% of physicians positive on visit interactions; 82% positive on work satisfaction. Strong and consistent.
- **NB (Net Benefits):** 15,791 aggregate hours saved vs. nonusers. DiD shows significant pajama time and after-hours reductions. Dose-response confirms concentration of benefit among heavy users. Patient experience positive: 56% better, 0% worse. Small increase in in-basket time is the only negative finding.
- **SerQ (Service Quality):** Vendor switch managed successfully. Rapid adoption of Vendor 2 with steeper uptake curve. Direct EHR integration seen as workflow enhancement. Sustained quality across both vendors.

## Corpus Context

- **Companion to Tierney 2024** — together these form the most longitudinal real-world dataset in the corpus: 10-week pilot + 63-week follow-up.
- **No COI** — entirely TPMG/Kaiser staff; no vendor funding, no industry relationships disclosed. One of the most credible large-scale reports in the corpus.
- **Age non-finding** is notable and unique in the corpus. Challenges generational adoption assumptions.
- **Vendor transition finding** is unique in the corpus — only paper describing what happens when a health system switches vendors mid-deployment. Supports the interpretation that *habits and behaviors are tool-agnostic*.
- **In-basket time increase** (+0.10 min, P=0.008) is the only negative efficiency finding — and is consistent with Holmgren 2026 and others noting that richer notes may generate more patient questions/follow-ups.
- **Dose-response confirmed** across corpus (see also Pearlman 2025, Lukac 2025, Omon 2025, Afshar 2025b).
- **15,791 hours aggregate** is a compelling ROI/workforce retention figure, but based on DiD vs. nonusers — actual counterfactual assumptions should be noted when citing.
