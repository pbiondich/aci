## Article Title
Ambient Artificial Intelligence Scribes to Alleviate the Burden of Clinical Documentation

## Author(s)
Tierney, Aaron A.; Gayre, Gregg; Hoberman, Brian; Mattern, Britt; Ballesca, Manuel; Kipnis, Patricia; Liu, Vincent; Lee, Kristine

## DOI
[10.1056/CAT.23.0404](https://doi.org/10.1056/CAT.23.0404)

## Journal / Source
NEJM Catalyst Innovations in Care Delivery | Vol. 5, No. 3 | February 21, 2024. The Permanente Medical Group (TPMG), Kaiser Permanente Northern California. Large-scale implementation study.

## Ambient Product(s) Evaluated
**Ambient AI scribe technology** (Nuance DAX Copilot). Smartphone microphone to transcribe encounters; does not retain audio recordings. EHR-integrated. Pilot enabled for 10,000 physicians and staff across diverse settings and specialties at TPMG in October 2023.

## Clinical Setting
The Permanente Medical Group (TPMG), Kaiser Permanente Northern California. Large-scale implementation across diverse settings and specialties. In 10 weeks since implementation, used by 3,442 physicians in as many as 303,266 patient encounters. 968 physicians enabled ambient AI scribes in ≥100 patient encounters; one physician in 1,210 encounters. Early evaluation metrics based on existing tool that evaluates quality of human-generated scribe notes.

## Evidence Level
Observational (large-scale implementation study with early evaluation metrics)

## Data Source(s)
Utilization data (303,266 encounters) | Early evaluation metrics based on human-generated scribe note quality tool | Statistical analyses of time spent in documentation and EHR

## Article Summary
This large-scale implementation study at TPMG enabled ambient AI scribe technology for 10,000 physicians and staff in October 2023. In 10 weeks, used by 3,442 physicians in 303,266 patient encounters across diverse specialties. Response from physicians who used the tool has been favorable — they cite the technology’s capability to facilitate more personal, meaningful, and effective patient interactions and to reduce the burden of after-hours clerical work. Early assessments of patient feedback have been positive, with some describing improved interaction with their physicians. Early evaluation metrics, based on an existing tool that evaluates the quality of human-generated scribe notes, find that ambient AI use produces high-quality clinical documentation for physicians’ editing. Further statistical analyses after AI scribe implementation also find that usage is linked with reduced time spent in documentation and in the EHR. Ongoing enhancements focused on direct EHR integration, improved capabilities for incorporating medical interpretation, and enhanced workflow personalization options for individual users. Despite early promise, careful attention must be paid to ensure the technology supports clinicians while optimizing output for accuracy, relevance, and alignment in the physician–patient relationship.

## Hypothesis
Explicit: ambient AI scribes have great potential to reduce documentation burden, enhance physician–patient encounters, and augment clinicians’ capabilities.
