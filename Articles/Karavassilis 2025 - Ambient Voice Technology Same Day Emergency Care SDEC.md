## Article Title
Ambient Voice Technology in Same Day Emergency Care: Enhancing Documentation Efficiency and Patient Flow

## Author(s)
Karavassilis, Maria; Youn, Sumin; Chernov, Dmitriy; Tatla, Esh; Ahmed, Iftikhar; Youn, Suhyun; Devendra, Devasenan

## DOI
[10.52964/AMJA.1007](https://doi.org/10.52964/AMJA.1007)

## Journal / Source
Acute Medicine | Vol. 24, No. 1 | 2025 | pp. 49–57. Service Improvement Pilot. West Hertfordshire Teaching Hospitals NHS Trust, UK. Acute Medicine Journal.

## Ambient Product(s) Evaluated
**Heidi Health** Ambient Voice Technology (AVT). Same product as Bracken 2025 (Paper 27) — Heidi Health, Australian company. Used in SDEC (Same Day Emergency Care) setting. Cerner Millennium EHR system.

## Clinical Setting
Same Day Emergency Care (SDEC) unit at West Hertfordshire Teaching Hospitals NHS Trust (UK). 20 clinicians across various specialties. 100 clinical case notes analyzed. 20 patients provided feedback. Clinicians had >3 years experience with Cerner Millennium EHR. Study period not specified — pilot implementation.

## Evidence Level
Quality Improvement (observational service improvement pilot)

## Data Source(s)
Direct time measurement (stopwatch) | Clinician survey (pre-AVT documentation practices, usability, burnout) | Patient survey (consultation experience) | Productivity data from practice management software | Word count for manual typing time estimation

## Article Summary
This UK service improvement pilot evaluated Heidi Health Ambient Voice Technology in a Same Day Emergency Care (SDEC) setting. 20 clinicians and 100 case notes analyzed. Key findings: AVT reduced documentation time by **85.8%** — average saving of 5.27 minutes per case (368s manual vs. 52s AVT, p<0.001). Clinicians reported high usability (mean 9.1/10), reduced administrative burden, and improved consultation flow. 90% of patients reported an enhanced consultation experience. 18/20 clinicians reported reduced burnout; 18/20 reported significant time savings. Most clinicians spent 0–30 minutes on manual documentation per consultation; 10 spent additional 0–30 minutes daily outside work hours. Confidence in manual note accuracy was moderate (common score 6/10). Missing or unclear information in manual records was a daily issue for 13 respondents. AVT was perceived to improve non-verbal communication and consultation flow. Patients felt clinicians were more attentive with AVT. This is one of the few papers in the corpus focused on SDEC/acute care settings rather than outpatient clinics.

## Hypothesis
To evaluate the impact of Heidi Health Ambient Voice Technology on documentation efficiency, clinician workload, and patient satisfaction in Same Day Emergency Care.

*📍 SDEC-specific study — Same Day Emergency Care (acute ambulatory care). Fills a gap in the corpus focused on outpatient clinic settings. UK NHS Trust setting.*
*📍 Heidi Health — same product as Bracken 2025 (Paper 27). Consistent findings: dramatic time savings (85.8% here vs. 79% in Bracken for progress notes). Cross-validation between two independent studies.*
*📍 Patient feedback included — 90% reported enhanced consultation experience. One of the few papers capturing patient perspective (compare Owens 2024 PDRQ-9 null result).*
*📍 Productivity not directly measured but implied through time savings (5.27 min/case). Clinicians reported significant time savings on documentation.*
*📍 Clinician survey shows high usability (9.1/10) and reduced burnout (18/20). Manual documentation burden is significant: 0–30 min per consultation for most, plus out-of-hours work.*
*📍 Note: This is a service improvement pilot — not a rigorous research study. No control group, self-reported outcomes, small clinician sample (n=20). Findings are directional rather than definitive.*
*📍 Cerner Millennium EHR context — different from Epic-heavy US studies. Shows AVT works across different EHR platforms.*
