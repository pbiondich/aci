## Author(s)

## Ambient Product(s) Evaluated
**Abridge** (explicitly: author affiliation includes "Abridge AI, Inc, Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania"). Multi-site study across 6 health systems. ⚠️ One co-author is an Abridge employee (institutional affiliation listed).

## Clinical Setting
6 health systems (sites 1–6): Yale New Haven Health/Yale Medicine, CHRISTUS Health (Irving TX), MemorialCare (Huntington Beach CA), The Christ Hospital (Cincinnati), Sutter Health (San Francisco), and one large academic medical center. 263 clinicians completed both pre- and post-intervention surveys. Mix: 88.2% MDs, 11% APPs; 63.9% academic, 34.2% medical group employed; all specialties including surgery (17.1%), adult specialty care (17.5%), OB/gyn (10.3%), pediatrics (14.4%), neurology/psychiatry (5.3%), primary care (35.3%). Mean years in practice 15.1.

## Evidence Level
Observational (pre/post mixed-methods — quantitative survey + qualitative components; multi-site)

## Data Source(s)
Survey (custom pre/post survey including validated burnout and cognitive load scales) | Qualitative (free-text responses)

## Article Summary
This large multi-site pre/post study of 263 clinicians across 6 health systems evaluated the impact of Abridge ambient AI scribes on burnout and administrative burden. Primary outcome: self-reported burnout (10-point scale). Burnout improved significantly: mean 4.59 → 4.12 (−0.47, p<.001); multivariable model: OR 0.26 [0.13, 0.54], p<.001 — i.e., 74% reduction in odds of burnout. Note-related cognitive task load improved dramatically: 7.10 → 4.46 (−2.64, p<.001). This is one of the largest and most diverse samples in the corpus (n=263, 6 sites, full specialty mix including surgery). The inclusion of an Abridge employee as a co-author is a COI to note. Editorial commentary published simultaneously in JAMA Network Open by Shah and Johnson ("Ambient AI Scribe Early Gains and Open Questions") and covered in JAMA News ("AI Scribe May Ease Administrative Burdens for Clinicians").

## Hypothesis
Explicit: to evaluate whether ambient AI scribe use reduces administrative burden and professional burnout among clinicians across health systems.

*📍 Notable: Largest and most diverse sample in the corpus for burnout outcomes (n=263, 6 sites, all specialty types). Burnout reduction OR 0.26 is the strongest effect size for burnout in the corpus. Multivariable adjustment increases confidence.*
*📍 COI: One co-author (Troup, Matt) is affiliated with Abridge AI, Inc. Disclosed in article information. The involvement of Abridge employees parallels Palm 2025 (Suki internal study). Disclosure is present but pattern worth noting across the field.*
*📍 Companion papers: Stults 2025 (Paper 10 in corpus) shares co-authors Stults and Jones — both are Sutter Health. This paper is the multi-site extension. Cited in JAMA News and received editorial commentary — high-impact paper.*
*📍 Cognitive task load finding (−2.64 points, p<.001) is the most striking secondary outcome — larger effect size than burnout itself. Suggests the cognitive relief mechanism may precede and exceed the burnout relief.*
