# Article Review

## Article Title
Artificial intelligence-driven digital scribes in clinical documentation: Pilot study assessing the impact on dermatologist workflow and patient encounters

## Author(s)
Cao, David Y.; Silkey, Jamie R.; Decker, Michael C.; Wanat, Karolyn A.

## DOI
[10.1016/j.jdin.2024.02.009](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jdin.2024.02.009)

## Journal / Source
JAAD International | Vol. 15 | June 2024 | pp. 149–151. Open Access. Medical College of Wisconsin. Dermatology-specific pilot study.

## Ambient Product(s) Evaluated
**Dragon Ambient eXperience (DAX)** (Nuance/Microsoft). Selected due to easy integration with Epic EHR and ability to convert encounters into specialty-specific notes. DAX is AI-driven and learns from clinician habits (language, formatting) after a virtual scribe has dictated notes.

## Clinical Setting
Department of Dermatology, Medical College of Wisconsin. 12 clinicians (10 dermatologists, 2 physician assistants) onboarded between February 2021 and January 2023. 10 elected to continue using DAX. Surveys completed 30-60 days after starting. Patient surveys following visits. Productivity metrics from Epic EHR Signal (time in notes per visit/week).

## Evidence Level
Observational (pilot study — pre/post EHR metrics + survey)

## Data Source(s)
EHR metrics (Epic Signal — time in notes per day, time in notes per appointment, time outside 7AM-5:30PM in EHR, note length, provider note contribution %) | Clinician survey | Patient survey

## Article Summary
This dermatology-specific pilot study at Medical College of Wisconsin evaluated DAX Copilot's impact on workflow and patient encounters. 12 clinicians onboarded; 10 continued use. EHR metrics showed significant reductions: time in notes per day from 54.6→42.2 min (p=.003); time in notes per appointment 6.5→5.1 min (p<.001); after-hours EHR time 35.5→28.1 min (p=.005). Note contribution percentage decreased by nearly half (96.7%→51.7%, p<.001), but note length increased (4412→4617 characters, p=.03). Low utilizers (<50%) and high utilizers (>70%) both saw improvements, with high utilizers having larger absolute reductions. Six clinicians completed the survey (60% response rate): 66.7% satisfied with documentation turnaround time; 83.3% would be "very disappointed" if DAX was unavailable; 83.3% felt it "significantly improved" overall quality of experience with patients. Patients reported positive perceptions of encounters during DAX use. Authors note potential cost advantage over in-person scribes ($1850/month software vs. $3050/month scribe including recruiting/training).

## Hypothesis
Explicit: to explore the use of DAX as a digital scribe in an academic and community-based dermatology setting.

*Reviewed by Iris | Source: JAAD International 2024 | DOI: 10.1016/j.jdin.2024.02.009 | Template v2.0 | Full text reviewed ✅*
*📍 Dermatology-specific pilot — adds specialty diversity to the corpus. 12 clinicians, 10 continued use.*
*📍 Objective EHR metrics show significant time reductions in notes, after-hours work, and overall EHR time. Note length increased slightly — consistent with many other papers.*
*📍 High clinician satisfaction: 83.3% would be "very disappointed" if DAX was unavailable. 83.3% felt it "significantly improved" quality of experience with patients.*
*📍 Cost comparison: Monthly software $1850 vs. human scribe $3050. Potential savings highlighted. This is one of the few papers discussing financial implications.*
*📍 Patient survey included — positive perceptions. One of few papers capturing patient perspective (compare Owens 2024 PDRQ-9 null result).*
*📍 Authors note limitations of small sample size and single institution. Data from pilot suggests digital scribes decrease documentation time, ease administrative burdens, and improve clinician and patient experience in dermatology.*
*📍 Note: This study was performed at a large academic institution — costs may not be generalizable.*
