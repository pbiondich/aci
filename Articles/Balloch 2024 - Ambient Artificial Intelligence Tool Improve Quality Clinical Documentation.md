Simulation study — outpatient consultations simulated with actors and clinicians. Documentation assessed using Sheffield Assessment Instrument for Letters (SAIL). Clinician experience measured through questionnaires and NASA Task Load Index. No real patients.

## Evidence Level
Observational (simulation study)

## Data Source(s)
Simulated consultations | SAIL for documentation quality | Questionnaires and NASA Task Load Index for clinician experience

## Article Summary
This simulation study evaluated an ambient AI tool's impact on clinical documentation quality and clinician experience. AI-produced documentation achieved higher SAIL scores than standard EHR practices. Consultations were 26.3% shorter on average without impacting patient interaction time. Clinicians reported an enhanced experience and reduced task load. The study concludes that the AI tool significantly improved documentation quality and operational efficiency in simulated consultations. Clinicians recognized its potential to improve note-taking processes.

## Hypothesis
To assess the clinical utility of an ambient AI tool in enhancing consultation experience and the completion of clinical documentation.
