# Article Review

---

## Article Title
ChatGPT’s Ability to Assist with Clinical Documentation: A Randomized Controlled Trial

## Author(s)
Baker, Hayden P.; Dwyer, Emma; Kalidoss, Senthooran; Hynes, Kelly; Wolf, Jennifer; Strelzow, Jason A.

## DOI
[10.5435/JAAOS-D-23-00474](https://doi.org/10.5435/JAAOS-D-23-00474)

## Journal / Source
Journal of the American Academy of Orthopaedic Surgeons (JAAOS) | Vol. 32, No. 3 | February 1, 2024 | pp. 123–129. Orthopaedic-specific study. University of Chicago. Randomized controlled pilot study.

## Ambient Product(s) Evaluated
**ChatGPT** (GPT-4, OpenAI) — used as a clinical documentation assistant. Not a dedicated ambient AI scribe like DAX or Abridge. Standardized prompt: “Please write a patient history of present illness including the following points:” followed by pertinent history points.

## Clinical Setting
University of Chicago, Department of Orthopaedic Surgery. **11 participants**: 4 medical students, 4 orthopaedic surgery residents (2 PGY3, 2 PGY4), 3 attending surgeons. 10 standardized patient histories with common orthopaedic conditions. Blinded, randomized controlled pilot study. Participants randomized into cohorts based on documentation technique (typing, dictation, ChatGPT). Each participant completed 3 HPIs using a different method for each. Time limit: 5 minutes to ask and collect details. 33 HPIs available for review.

## Evidence Level
RCT (blinded, randomized controlled pilot study)

## Data Source(s)
Direct time measurement | Word count | History items included | PDQI-9 (9-item Physician Documentation Quality Instrument, 0–45) | Global rating tool (1–5) | Error/hallucination detection

## Article Summary
This orthopaedic-specific RCT is one of the few randomized controlled studies in the corpus. 11 participants (students, residents, attendings) documented 3 HPIs each using typing, dictation, and ChatGPT. Key findings: (1) **Dictation fastest** (43s), ChatGPT intermediate (69s), typing slowest (97s); (2) **ChatGPT produced significantly longer notes** (136 words vs. 90 dictation, 68 typing); (3) **ChatGPT had highest PDQI-9 and overall rating scores** — significantly better than typing and dictation; (4) ChatGPT included **erroneous/fictitious history points in 36% of documents** (4/11); (5) Poor inter-rater agreement on PDQI-9 and global rating between reviewers (ICC 0.1–0.19). Efficiency score (PDQI-9 per minute): dictation 51.1, ChatGPT 34.9, typing 21.5. Authors conclude ChatGPT has potential to improve clinical documentation by producing more comprehensive and organized HPIs, but additional studies are needed to address privacy, bias, and accuracy concerns. Hallucination rate of 36% is a significant safety signal.

## Hypothesis
To investigate ChatGPT’s ability to assist with writing a history of present illness based on standardized patient histories compared with histories generated solely by healthcare professionals.

*📍 Orthopaedic-specific RCT — one of the few randomized controlled studies in the corpus. Small pilot (n=11 participants, 33 HPIs).*
*📍 ChatGPT hallucination rate of 36% is one of the highest direct measurements in the corpus — clinically significant safety concern. Hallucinations included adding physical exam findings and history details not in the standardized script.*
*📍 Efficiency paradox: Dictation was fastest and had highest efficiency score (PDQI-9 per minute), but ChatGPT produced longer, higher-quality notes. Trade-off between speed and quality.*
*📍 Poor inter-rater agreement on PDQI-9 (ICC 0.1) and global rating (ICC 0.19) — consistent with other papers noting subjectivity in note quality assessment. This is a methodological limitation of using PDQI-9 for AI vs. human note comparison.*
*📍 Authors conclude ChatGPT has potential but additional studies needed for privacy, bias, and accuracy. This is one of the more cautious papers in the corpus regarding AI documentation tools.*
*📍 Note: This study uses ChatGPT as a documentation assistant with a standardized prompt — not an ambient AI scribe that listens passively during the encounter. Different technology from DAX/Abridge/Suki.*
